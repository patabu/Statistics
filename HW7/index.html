Homework 7 (P): Discussion on Sample Size and Distribution

1. Behavior with Few Samples

Empirical Distribution Variability:

  • When the number of samples is small, the empirical distribution exhibits higher variability.
  • This happens because each sample has a significant impact on the distribution, and random fluctuations are more pronounced.

Noticeable Differences Between Distributions:

  • The empirical distribution tends to deviate more significantly from the theoretical distribution.
  • Empirical mean and variance often differ substantially from their theoretical counterparts.

2. Increasing the Number of Samples

Effect on the Graph:

  • As the number of samples increases, the empirical distribution progressively aligns with the theoretical distribution.
  • Random variability is reduced as more samples "smooth out" local discrepancies.

Means and Variances:

  • The empirical mean starts converging toward the theoretical mean, as guaranteed by the Law of Large Numbers.
  • Similarly, the empirical variance approaches the theoretical variance, better capturing the data's spread.

3. Results with Many Samples (e.g., 5000)

Empirical vs. Theoretical Distribution:

  • With 5000 samples, the empirical distribution closely follows the theoretical distribution.
  • The green bars (empirical) and yellow bars (theoretical) are almost overlapping.

Convergence of Mean and Variance:

  • The empirical mean (7.837) and the theoretical mean (7.967) are very close.
  • The empirical variance (18.662) is nearly identical to the theoretical variance (18.604).

Overall Convergence:

  • This behavior is explained by the Central Limit Theorem (CLT), which ensures that with enough samples, the empirical distribution becomes a faithful representation of the theoretical one.

4. Conclusions on the Effect of Sample Size

With Few Samples:

  • The empirical distribution may deviate significantly from the theoretical one.
  • Empirical means and variances show more noticeable discrepancies.

With Many Samples:

  • The empirical distribution becomes increasingly similar to the theoretical distribution.
  • Empirical means and variances converge toward the theoretical values.

Limits and Observations:

  • Although 5000 samples represent a significant number, small discrepancies remain, which would diminish further with an even larger sample size.
  • The marginal gain decreases as the number of samples increases: beyond a certain point, adding more samples yields negligible improvements.
